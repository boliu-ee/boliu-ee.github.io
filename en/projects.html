Neural Optical Communication Equalizer Design for 800 Gb/s PAM4 CWDM4Jun 2021 – Dec 2022

Ph.D. project

  • Proposed a multi-task-learning-based shared equalizer architecture across multiple wavelengths to avoid repeated per-link equalizer designs.
  • Introduced a multi-output-learning-based parallel equalizer architecture to address hardware parallelization demands caused by optical/electrical throughput mismatch.
  • At 45 nm and 1 GHz, achieved comparable performance to conventional methods while reducing area by 77% and power by 61%; bias-only reconfiguration enables wavelength adaptation.
Efficient MAC Array Design and Neural Network AcceleratorJan 2023 – Nov 2024

Ph.D. project

  • Designed a generalized weighted-encoding MAC array to address large area and high power consumption of multiply-accumulate units in neural-network deployment.
  • In a 15 nm, 5 GHz, 64×64 MAC array implementation, reduced power by 43.10% and area by 23.69% compared with a conventional systolic array.
VLSI Design and Implementation of High-Accuracy Voice Activity Detection under Low SNRNov 2018 – May 2020

Master’s thesis

  • Designed a low-power VLSI solution for speech activity detection under high background noise, targeting both accuracy and hardware cost.
  • Compared with prior work, improved detection accuracy to 85%–94%, while reducing power by 30% and area by 26%.
